Heroes


Edward Kenway

An extremely skilled pirate who was almost consumed by his own ambition. His slow, yet not too late, realization that friendship and relations matter more than materialistic pirate booty was a result of an adventurous and tumultous life that saw him engage with swashbucklers, assassins, templars, otherworldy artefacts and the notorious East India Company. But, also part of his fascinating story were heartwarming friendships, light moments and melancholy goodbyes. Ultimately, Edward had an epiphany which lead him to abandon his dangerous life and settle with his daughter peacefully whose mother he had abandoned in an insatiable desire "to make it big".

Geralt of Rivia

His is one of the most engaging and enchanting stories. From the abstract philosophy of the relativity of different evils and the difficult choices one must make to slaying monsters, monstrous in spirit yet only sometimes in shape, making strong bonds with all kinds of beings, visiting fantasical lands, from the beautiful Beauclair to despairing Velen, and of course making memories all along the way. Hearing of his adventures one is uncontrollably attached to its people invloved and lands ventured upon. Truly makes one visit another reality.

Lara Croft

A thrilling tale of survival set in the mythical island of Yamatai. Apart from the ruthless and barabaric armed thugs of the island that are under the influence of a pathological and fanatic leader that is convinced that a young girl(Lara's friend) must be sacrificed for their redemption, this adventure also traces the internal jounrey Lara goes through from a scared and ingenous girl to a confident and bold women who takes down hordes of thugs and an even undead giants with the rapid firing of her assault rifle. Saving one's own skin, rescuing friends, experiencing loss and of course the magic and exoticism of mysterious tombs, this story has them all.
